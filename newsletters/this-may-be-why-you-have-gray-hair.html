Have gray hair? This may be why.

Gray Hair Causes

January 21st, 2021
Expert Susan McWilliams (M.D, S.D)

Graying hair is caused by the loss of pigment cells called melanin. The age you lose these cells can depend on many factors. One factor is genetics. If your parents or grandparents had hair that greyed early, it is likely that you will have gray hair at a younger age as well. One researcher also says that "while genetics can be a factor in the age gray hair comes at, stress plays a big role in causing gray hair as well."

Studies have shown that stress can cause the depletion of cells in hair follicles. Stress may also lead to sleep problems and other health issues that can affect your hair. Various other disorders may affect your hair health. Examples of these disorders include Autoimmune disease, Thyroid disorder, and vitamin deficiency. Gray hair typically happens to everyone as they age, however gray hair at ages under 30 should be checked with your doctor.
